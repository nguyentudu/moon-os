# Decision Tracking v1

## Purpose

Decision tracking knowledge helps Moon preserve why choices were made, what assumptions supported them, and what consequences followed.

## Core Concepts

- decision: a choice that changes direction, priority, commitment, or interpretation.
- rationale: the stated reason a decision was made.
- assumption: a belief or condition treated as true when deciding.
- consequence: an observed or expected result of a decision.
- unresolved decision: a pending choice that continues to affect action or attention.

## Lifecycle

A decision may emerge as a question, become framed, gather evidence, receive rationale, be made, be acted on, be revisited, be reversed, or become obsolete. Assumptions may later be confirmed, weakened, or invalidated.

Moon should preserve decision context so future reasoning can distinguish changed reality from repeated uncertainty.

## Signals

- reversals: changes that undo or contradict prior decisions.
- unresolved decisions: open questions, blocked next steps, or repeated deferrals.
- rationale statements: explanations of why a path was chosen.
- assumption language: phrases such as "if", "because", "assuming", or "given that".
- consequence evidence: outcomes, feedback, costs, or new constraints.

## Risks

- repeated mistakes: prior rationale and consequences are forgotten.
- forgotten context: later choices are made without knowing why earlier choices happened.
- stale assumptions: old assumptions continue guiding action after conditions change.
- false certainty: decisions are remembered without their uncertainty.

## Continuity Relevance

Decisions are continuity anchors. They explain why the current state exists and help future reasoning avoid flattening history into isolated facts.

## Future Metrics Candidates

- decision pressure score
- unresolved decision count
- assumption risk score
- reversal frequency
- rationale completeness score
- consequence visibility score
