# Relationship Lifecycle v1

## Purpose

Relationship lifecycle knowledge helps Moon understand how relationships become active, latent, declining, or ended across personal and professional contexts.

## Core Concepts

- active relationship: a relationship with current communication, collaboration, care, or mutual relevance.
- latent relationship: a relationship with history and possible future relevance but little current activity.
- declining relationship: a relationship showing reduced reciprocity, communication, trust, or shared context.
- ended relationship: a relationship explicitly closed or strongly evidenced as no longer active.
- reciprocity: evidence of mutual exchange, attention, support, or collaboration.

## Lifecycle

A relationship may begin through contact, collaboration, shared context, or explicit connection. It may become active through repeated exchange. It may become latent through reduced activity without rupture. It may decline through asymmetry, unresolved tension, or fading relevance. It may end through explicit closure, rupture, or durable absence.

Relationship state should be treated with caution because evidence may be incomplete.

## Signals

- communication frequency: messages, calls, meetings, or replies.
- collaboration: shared projects, tasks, decisions, or recurring coordination.
- reciprocity: mutual initiation and response.
- tone shift: changes in warmth, trust, friction, or distance.
- explicit status: statements about closeness, conflict, closure, or boundaries.

## Risks

- unnoticed decay: a relationship weakens without conscious awareness.
- false importance: old or frequent contact is mistaken for current significance.
- overreach: Moon surfaces sensitive relationship context without sufficient consent.
- misread silence: absence of contact is interpreted too strongly.

## Continuity Relevance

Relationships shape commitments, emotional context, collaboration, identity, and trust. Continuity requires understanding relational context without treating people as tasks.
