# Goal Drift v1

## Purpose

Goal drift knowledge helps Moon notice when stated goals and lived behavior diverge. It exists to support awareness without deciding what the user should pursue.

## Core Concepts

- goal: a desired outcome, direction, standard, or identity-aligned aim.
- goal alignment: evidence that decisions, time, effort, and commitments support the goal.
- goal drift: a gradual divergence between the stated goal and actual behavior or allocation.
- goal abandonment: explicit or strongly evidenced discontinuation of a goal.
- competing goal: another goal that draws attention, time, or decisions away from the original goal.

## Lifecycle

A goal may be declared, explored, refined, pursued, paused, drifted from, abandoned, or completed. Drift may be temporary and adaptive, or it may indicate that the goal no longer matches current reality.

Moon should treat drift as a signal for awareness, not a failure judgment.

## Signals

- effort allocation: work, notes, artifacts, and repeated actions connected to the goal.
- calendar allocation: time reserved or actually spent toward the goal.
- decision alignment: choices that support or contradict the stated goal.
- language shift: the user speaks differently about priority, desire, obligation, or relevance.
- competing commitments: new obligations that reduce available effort.

## Risks

- invisible drift: divergence accumulates without being noticed.
- competing goals: multiple goals silently compete for the same attention and resources.
- stale aspiration: an old goal remains named but no longer has meaningful support.
- shame framing: drift is interpreted as failure rather than information.

## Continuity Relevance

Goals connect past intention to present action and future direction. Tracking drift helps Moon preserve agency by making alignment visible without prescribing decisions.

## Future Metrics Candidates

- goal drift score
- goal alignment score
- effort allocation consistency
- calendar alignment score
- competing goal pressure
