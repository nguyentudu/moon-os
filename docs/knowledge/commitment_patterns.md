# Commitment Patterns v1

## Purpose

Commitment pattern knowledge helps Moon understand promises, obligations, responsibilities, and follow-through over time. It exists to reduce forgotten obligations and commitment debt.

## Core Concepts

- commitment: an explicit or strongly evidenced intent to do something.
- obligation: a responsibility created by role, agreement, expectation, or dependency.
- promise: a commitment made to another person or group.
- responsibility: an area of ongoing accountability, even without a single due date.
- commitment debt: accumulated unresolved commitments that increase cognitive load or trust risk.

## Lifecycle

A commitment may be created, clarified, scheduled, acted on, delayed, renegotiated, fulfilled, transferred, cancelled, or forgotten. Some commitments are one-time actions. Others become ongoing responsibilities.

Moon should distinguish active commitments from expired, completed, or renegotiated ones.

## Signals

- due dates: explicit deadlines, expected dates, or time-bound language.
- follow-ups: reminders, check-ins, replies, or promised next actions.
- inactivity: lack of action after commitment creation.
- dependency language: another person or project depends on the user.
- completion evidence: delivery, confirmation, closure note, or fulfilled outcome.

## Risks

- commitment debt: too many unresolved obligations accumulate.
- forgotten obligations: commitments disappear from attention without closure.
- implied promises: vague language creates expectations without explicit agreement.
- stale urgency: old commitments keep demanding attention after they are no longer relevant.

## Continuity Relevance

Commitments connect intention to trust. Tracking their patterns helps Moon support reliability while respecting human agency and attention limits.

## Future Metrics Candidates

- commitment risk score
- commitment debt load
- follow-up latency
- obligation clarity score
- promise sensitivity score
- closure confidence
