# Continuity Metrics Reference v1

## Purpose

This reference lists future metric candidates for Moon OS continuity reasoning. It does not define formulas, scoring logic, thresholds, data models, or implementation behavior.

## Core Concepts

- metric candidate: a possible future dimension for evaluating continuity.
- signal family: a group of observable indicators that may later inform a metric.
- scoring dimension: a conceptual axis that may support future graph or reasoning work.
- non-formula constraint: no calculation is defined in this layer.

## Lifecycle

Metric candidates begin as conceptual names connected to knowledge domains. Later layers may validate, refine, reject, or operationalize them. Until then, they remain references only.

## Signals

Future metric work may inspect signals already named in the knowledge domains, including activity, ownership, effort allocation, due dates, reciprocity, reversals, revisit frequency, interruption cost, and boundary conflicts.

## Risks

- premature quantification: assigning formulas before the domain is understood.
- false precision: treating incomplete continuity evidence as exact measurement.
- metric overreach: optimizing scores instead of awareness and user agency.
- attention distortion: surfacing items because they score highly rather than because they matter.

## Continuity Relevance

Metrics can help future Moon layers rank, compare, and reason about continuity. They must remain subordinate to law, trust, attention, evidence, and user agency.
