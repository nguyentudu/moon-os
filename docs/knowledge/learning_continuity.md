# Learning Continuity v1

## Purpose

Learning continuity knowledge helps Moon understand how research, study, experimentation, and skill development persist or fragment over time.

## Core Concepts

- learning thread: an ongoing line of inquiry, study, practice, or research.
- knowledge retention: the degree to which learned material remains accessible and usable.
- research continuity: preserved context across sources, questions, findings, and next steps.
- application: use of learning in decisions, projects, explanations, or created artifacts.
- fragmentation: related learning scattered across disconnected sources or sessions.

## Lifecycle

A learning thread may start with curiosity, become structured through questions and sources, deepen through repeated revisits, produce applied knowledge, pause, fragment, or conclude. It may later reactivate when a project, decision, or question makes it relevant again.

## Signals

- revisit frequency: repeated engagement with the same topic, source, or question.
- application: use of learning in work, writing, decisions, or teaching.
- source accumulation: notes, documents, links, highlights, or project artifacts.
- unanswered questions: explicit unknowns or research gaps.
- synthesis evidence: summaries, models, principles, or comparisons.

## Risks

- abandoned learning: promising inquiry stops without closure or capture.
- fragmented knowledge: related ideas remain scattered and hard to recover.
- false mastery: exposure is mistaken for retention or application.
- context decay: the reason a source mattered is lost.

## Continuity Relevance

Learning shapes future capability and judgment. Preserving learning continuity helps Moon connect old inquiry to new relevance without surfacing trivia.

## Future Metrics Candidates

- learning continuity score
- research thread activity
- retention confidence
- application frequency
- fragmentation risk
- unanswered question pressure
