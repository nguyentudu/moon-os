# Economic Metric Definitions v1

## Purpose

Define the first economic metrics for Moon Decision Memory and Team Continuity.

Metrics are measurement instruments, not conclusions.

## Core Metrics

### Time To Context

Definition:

Time required for a person to understand enough context to answer why a system exists or why a decision was made.

Economic relevance:

Lower Time To Context may reduce onboarding cost, interruption cost, and time wasted rereading historical material.

### Time To Contribution

Definition:

Time required for a new or returning contributor to make a useful contribution without repeating old mistakes.

Economic relevance:

Lower Time To Contribution may increase team throughput and reduce senior-person support burden.

### Understanding Quality

Measures:

- answer completeness
- rationale accuracy
- evidence usage
- tradeoff awareness
- current validity awareness

Economic relevance:

Fast answers are not valuable if they are wrong, shallow, or missing constraints.

### Decision Recall

Measures whether a participant can remember:

- decision
- reason
- evidence
- consequence
- current validity

Economic relevance:

Better recall can reduce rework and repeated debate.

### Mistake Avoidance

Measures whether Moon helps prevent:

- repeating rejected decisions
- missing known constraints
- ignoring prior rationale
- reopening settled issues without new evidence

Economic relevance:

Avoided mistakes can translate into avoided engineering time, delivery delay, trust loss, or coordination cost.

### Hours Saved

Model:

baseline time - Moon-assisted time

Economic relevance:

Hours saved becomes economically meaningful only when tied to role cost, avoided delay, or opportunity value.

## Measurement Boundary

Reliability metrics do not equal economic metrics.

Reliable classification helps make Decision Memory safer to expand, but does not by itself prove economic value.
