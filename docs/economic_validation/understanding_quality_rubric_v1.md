# Understanding Quality Rubric v1

## Purpose

Score whether participants understand Moon decisions well enough for the answer to be economically useful.

## Rating Scale

Use:

- Strong
- Acceptable
- Borderline
- Poor

## Dimensions

### Answer Completeness

Strong:

Answers decision, reason, consequence, and current validity.

Poor:

Mentions only files or surface facts.

### Rationale Accuracy

Strong:

Correctly explains why a decision was made.

Poor:

Invents rationale or confuses adjacent decisions.

### Evidence Usage

Strong:

Cites relevant decision records or source documents.

Poor:

Provides no evidence or irrelevant evidence.

### Tradeoff Awareness

Strong:

Names what was not chosen and why.

Poor:

Treats the decision as obvious or context-free.

### Current Validity Awareness

Strong:

States whether the decision still appears valid, conditional, stale, or unknown.

Poor:

Assumes old decisions are automatically current.

## Scoring Boundary

Understanding Quality should be scored independently from time.

Fast but wrong answers do not count as economic value.
