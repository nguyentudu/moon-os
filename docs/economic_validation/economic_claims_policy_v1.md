# Economic Claims Policy v1

## Purpose

Define what Moon may claim about economic value.

## Claim Levels

### NO_CLAIM

Use when no participant data exists.

Allowed wording:

No economic evidence has been collected.

### EARLY_SIGNAL

Use when there is a small sample or incomplete controls, but directional improvement exists.

Allowed wording:

Early evidence suggests possible improvement.

Not allowed:

ROI, productivity proof, market proof.

### VALIDATED_SIGNAL

Use when multiple participants show repeatable improvement and quality scores are acceptable.

Allowed wording:

Repeated study evidence suggests Moon improves the measured workflow.

### ECONOMIC_EVIDENCE

Use when measured time saved or mistake avoided has a credible baseline.

Allowed wording:

Moon produced measured time savings or mistake avoidance in this study context.

### BUSINESS_CASE_READY

Use when economic evidence maps to cost, revenue, or risk reduction.

Required:

- credible cost model
- role-based assumptions
- repeatability
- quality threshold
- limitations disclosed

## Hard Claim Rules

- No economic claim without data.
- TTC Signal remains preliminary until controlled studies exist.
- Reliability evidence is not economic evidence.
- Boundary problems must remain visible.
- Decision Expansion remains blocked unless separately approved.
- Evidence and interpretation must be separated.
