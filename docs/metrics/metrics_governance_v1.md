# Metrics Governance v1

## Purpose

Metrics Governance defines how Moon OS may use metrics without allowing measurement to override law, trust, attention, or human agency.

## Principle 1

Metrics inform.

Metrics do not decide.

## Principle 2

High score does not guarantee relevance.

A metric can indicate risk or momentum without proving that the item deserves attention now.

## Principle 3

Low score does not guarantee surfacing.

Weakness, inactivity, or risk must still pass evidence, trust, attention, and reasoning gates before surfacing.

## Principle 4

Trust overrides metrics.

If a metric conflicts with consent, recall boundaries, sensitivity, or trust safety, trust wins.

## Principle 5

Attention overrides engagement.

Metrics must support awareness, not usage time, notification volume, or engagement loops.

## Principle 6

Reasoning may challenge metrics.

Future reasoning layers may disagree with, contextualize, suppress, or downgrade metric signals when evidence, uncertainty, user boundaries, or timing require it.

## Principle 7

Metrics must remain explainable.

Every metric used to support surfacing should be explainable through source, evidence, related graph objects, relevance, uncertainty, and why now.

## Required Alignment

Metrics must reference and remain subordinate to:

- [Personal Continuity Manifest](../../PERSONAL_CONTINUITY_MANIFEST.md)
- [Trust Governance](../../TRUST_GOVERNANCE.md)
- [Attention Constitution](../../ATTENTION_CONSTITUTION.md)

## Non-Implementation Boundary

This governance layer does not define formulas, thresholds, scoring engines, ranking algorithms, recommendation systems, agents, notifications, storage, or user interfaces.
