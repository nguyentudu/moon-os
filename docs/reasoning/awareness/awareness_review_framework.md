# Awareness Review Framework v1

## Purpose

The Awareness Review Framework defines how Awareness QA reviews a continuity concern after the Continuity Director.

This is a review structure only. It is not a UI, ranking engine, notification system, reminder system, or agent workflow.

## Review Questions

### What continuity concern exists?

Identify the concern and the Continuity Director verdict.

### Why does it matter?

Explain the continuity significance.

### Why today?

Explain why the concern deserves attention now.

If this cannot be answered, the candidate must not advance.

### What evidence exists?

Identify source-linked evidence.

Evidence should remain visible and explainable.

### What attention cost exists?

Describe the cognitive, emotional, or interruption cost.

### What awareness value exists?

Describe how surfacing would improve awareness, decision quality, continuity, or understanding.

### What uncertainty exists?

Expose missing evidence, weak confidence, timing uncertainty, trust uncertainty, or competing priorities.

### Should it advance?

Choose an Awareness outcome:

- SUPPRESS
- DEFER
- SURFACE
- PRIORITIZE
- REJECT

## Review Output

An Awareness QA review should include:

- continuity concern
- Continuity Director verdict
- evidence summary
- attention relevance factors
- attention cost
- awareness value
- uncertainty
- trust status
- outcome
- why today explanation

## Non-Implementation Boundary

This framework does not define storage, retrieval, UI, ranking, notifications, reminders, scoring logic, or action execution.
