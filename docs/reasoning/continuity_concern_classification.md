# Continuity Concern Classification v1

## Purpose

This reference defines concern types the Continuity Director may identify.

Concern types are reasoning categories only. They are not alerts, reminders, notifications, or implementation rules.

## Project Momentum Risk

- Purpose: identify meaningful loss of project movement.
- Signals: low momentum, reduced activity, weak ownership, stale project context.
- Typical indicators: no recent artifacts, unresolved decisions, inactive commitments, dormant project state.
- Risks: hidden abandonment, false momentum, context loss.
- Continuity impact: project continuity may be weakening.
- Required evidence: project graph object, activity evidence, source-linked metric indicators.

## Goal Drift

- Purpose: identify divergence between stated goal and observed effort or decisions.
- Signals: goal drift metric, reduced effort allocation, competing priorities, contradictory decisions.
- Typical indicators: goal active but projects or commitments no longer support it.
- Risks: invisible drift, forgotten goals, misaligned attention.
- Continuity impact: intention and action may be separating.
- Required evidence: goal graph object, linked projects or decisions, evidence of allocation change.

## Commitment Risk

- Purpose: identify obligations that may be at risk.
- Signals: commitment risk, due dates, inactivity, unresolved follow-ups.
- Typical indicators: active commitment with no closure evidence, dependency on another person, stale follow-up.
- Risks: commitment debt, silent failure, trust damage.
- Continuity impact: reliability and trust may be affected.
- Required evidence: commitment graph object, source, evidence link, confidence.

## Relationship Decay

- Purpose: identify possible weakening of relationship continuity.
- Signals: reduced communication, reduced reciprocity, boundary changes, relationship activity decline.
- Typical indicators: active relationship becoming latent or declining.
- Risks: unnoticed decay, false importance, stale assumptions.
- Continuity impact: relationship context may require awareness, but only with care.
- Required evidence: relationship graph object and direct or strong supporting evidence.

## Decision Instability

- Purpose: identify unstable, reversed, or unresolved decisions.
- Signals: decision pressure, reversal frequency, conflicting actions, unresolved choices.
- Typical indicators: repeated reversals, stale assumptions, blocked projects.
- Risks: repeated mistakes, forgotten rationale, unresolved decisions.
- Continuity impact: current direction may lack stable reasoning context.
- Required evidence: decision graph object, rationale or reversal evidence, affected entities.

## Learning Fragmentation

- Purpose: identify learning threads losing continuity.
- Signals: low learning continuity, paused topics, scattered sources, little application.
- Typical indicators: abandoned learning topic, repeated restarts, no synthesis.
- Risks: fragmented learning, topic abandonment, knowledge decay.
- Continuity impact: knowledge may become hard to recover or apply.
- Required evidence: LearningTopic or Thread graph object with source-linked learning signals.

## Attention Overload

- Purpose: identify excessive or noisy attention demand.
- Signals: attention load, attention debt, dense competing concerns.
- Typical indicators: many active concerns, repeated unresolved loops, weak signal clutter.
- Risks: awareness overload, notification inflation, cognitive load increase.
- Continuity impact: Moon may need to reduce rather than increase surfacing.
- Required evidence: attention metrics and graph objects showing unresolved competing demands.

## Trust Sensitivity

- Purpose: identify cases where trust risk may constrain reasoning or surfacing.
- Signals: trust risk, consent uncertainty, recall sensitivity, boundary conflict.
- Typical indicators: sensitive memory, relationship rupture, health context, identity-sensitive event.
- Risks: trust rupture, overreach, hidden retrieval.
- Continuity impact: trust may require silence even when continuity relevance exists.
- Required evidence: consent boundary, sensitivity evidence, source, confidence.

## Context Loss

- Purpose: identify loss of rationale, source, or continuity context.
- Signals: weak context preservation, missing rationale, fragmented documents.
- Typical indicators: decisions without rationale, projects without next steps, memories without usable links.
- Risks: repeated mistakes, restart cost, false certainty.
- Continuity impact: future reasoning may become unreliable.
- Required evidence: graph object with missing or weak context evidence.

## Hidden Abandonment

- Purpose: identify objects that appear open but may no longer be alive.
- Signals: dormant project, drifting goal, inactive commitment, no ownership signal.
- Typical indicators: no activity, no closure, no owner action, stale status.
- Risks: commitment debt, false continuity, attention debt.
- Continuity impact: unresolved open loops may distort awareness.
- Required evidence: lifecycle state, activity absence evidence, source-linked history.
