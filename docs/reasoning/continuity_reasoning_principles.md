# Continuity Reasoning Principles v1

## Purpose

These principles govern how the Continuity Director evaluates continuity significance.

## Principle 1

Evidence before concern.

A concern cannot exist without evidence.

## Principle 2

Continuity before recency.

Recent signals are not automatically more important than older continuity-significant signals.

## Principle 3

Importance before novelty.

Novel or interesting signals are insufficient unless they matter to awareness, decisions, commitments, relationships, trust, or continuity.

## Principle 4

Explanation before escalation.

If the Director cannot explain why something matters and why now, it should not advance.

## Principle 5

Trust before awareness.

Trust boundaries, sensitivity, and consent override awareness opportunities.

## Principle 6

Uncertainty must be visible.

Confidence, missing evidence, conflicting evidence, and ambiguity must be exposed.

## Principle 7

Silence is valid.

No recommendation, PASS, or UNKNOWN can be correct outputs.

## Principle 8

Reasoning may challenge metrics.

Metrics are indicators, not conclusions. Reasoning may downgrade, suppress, or question metric signals when evidence, trust, attention, or context requires it.
