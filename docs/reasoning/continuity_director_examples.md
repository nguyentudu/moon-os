# Continuity Director Examples v1

## Purpose

These examples illustrate how the Continuity Director may evaluate continuity significance.

Examples only. No implementation is defined.

## Case 1: Project Alpha

### Inputs

- Project: Project Alpha
- Metric signal: Momentum low
- Metric signal: Goal alignment high
- Graph signal: recent activity absent
- Evidence: project activity history and linked goal evidence

### Review

What continuity object is involved?

Project Alpha.

What evidence exists?

Source-linked project history and recent absence of activity.

What metrics contributed?

Momentum and Goal Alignment.

What continuity risk exists?

Project Momentum Risk and possible Hidden Abandonment.

Why does it matter?

The project appears important to an active goal but may be losing movement.

Why now?

Recent activity is absent while alignment remains high.

What uncertainty exists?

The absence of activity may reflect an intentional pause.

### Verdict

CONCERN

### Note

Worth awareness review. Not an instruction to resume the project.

## Case 2: Relationship Inactive

### Inputs

- Relationship: inactive relationship
- Evidence: no recent communication
- Missing evidence: no direct evidence of decline

### Review

What continuity object is involved?

Relationship.

What evidence exists?

Limited evidence of inactivity only.

What metrics contributed?

Relationship Activity.

What continuity risk exists?

Possible Relationship Decay, but evidence is weak.

Why does it matter?

Relationship continuity may be changing, but the current evidence is insufficient.

Why now?

No strong why-now justification exists.

What uncertainty exists?

No evidence of decline, rupture, boundary shift, or importance.

### Verdict

WATCH

### Note

Monitor conceptually. Do not surface as a concern.
