# Constitutional Failure Modes v1

## Purpose

This document defines failure modes the validation suite should detect.

## False Concern

Why it is dangerous:

It creates anxiety and attention load without sufficient continuity value.

Layer failed:

Continuity Director or Metrics interpretation.

## False Silence

Why it is dangerous:

Moon misses a meaningful continuity risk that deserved awareness.

Layer failed:

Continuity Director, Awareness QA, or Today visibility.

## Trust Violation

Why it is dangerous:

It damages the user's trust relationship with Moon.

Layer failed:

Trust QA or Today Surface Contract.

## Attention Inflation

Why it is dangerous:

It turns awareness into noise.

Layer failed:

Awareness QA or Today Surface Contract.

## Awareness Without Evidence

Why it is dangerous:

Moon presents inference as fact.

Layer failed:

Graph Evidence Model, Continuity Director, or Task Writer.

## Memory Without Consent

Why it is dangerous:

It violates consent before recall.

Layer failed:

Trust QA.

## Over-Surfacing

Why it is dangerous:

Too many valid items can still harm attention and clarity.

Layer failed:

Awareness QA or Today Surface Contract.

## Under-Surfacing

Why it is dangerous:

Important continuity risks remain invisible.

Layer failed:

Continuity Director or Awareness QA.

## Confidence Misrepresentation

Why it is dangerous:

Uncertain claims appear more certain than they are.

Layer failed:

Confidence Model, Task Writer, or Today Surface Contract.
