# Round 1 vs Round 2 Comparison v1

## Purpose

Compare Round 1 and Round 2 evidence without claiming production reliability.

## Round 1 Baseline

- reviewer count: 3
- valid classifications: 150
- Decision labels: 135
- Not Decision labels: 0
- Unclear labels: 15
- missing classifications: 0
- all-3 exact agreement: 40/50 (80.0%)

## Round 2 Evidence

- reviewer count: 3
- valid classifications: 149
- Decision labels: 122
- Not Decision labels: 16
- Unclear labels: 11
- missing classifications: 1
- all-3 exact agreement on comparable excerpts: 34/49 (69.4%)

## Did Not Decision Appear?

YES.

Round 1: 0 Not Decision labels.

Round 2: 16 Not Decision labels.

## Did Boundary Discrimination Improve?

YES, partially.

Evidence:

- Not Decision became available in reviewer behavior.
- Several expected Not Decision excerpts received Not Decision labels.
- Reviewers still used Not Decision inconsistently, including on expected Decision excerpts.

## Did Ambiguity Clusters Persist?

YES.

Persisting ambiguity appears around:

- metrics and counts
- deployment/status checks
- claim boundaries
- no-backend/no-submission constraints
- explanation requests signaled by comments like Tại sao

## Interpretation

Round 2 improved boundary evidence but did not settle the Decision Definition boundary.
