# Reliability Claims v1

## Purpose

Separate claims supported by reviewer evidence from claims that must be withheld.

## Evidence-Supported Claims Allowed

- Three reviewer submissions were imported.
- Reviewer A has a blank reviewer ID and should be treated as malformed metadata, not discarded evidence.
- All 150 excerpt classifications are present.
- No reviewer used not decision.
- Pairwise simple agreement was calculated for A vs B, A vs C, and B vs C.
- Exact three-reviewer agreement occurred on 40 of 50 excerpts.
- At least one reviewer disagreed on 10 excerpts.
- EV-029 was classified unclear by all three reviewers.
- Most disagreement clustered around excerpts that look like metrics, status notes, shorthand inventory items, or context-poor rationale statements.
- Reviewer B and Reviewer C aligned more closely with each other than either did with Reviewer A in this sample.

## Claims Withheld

- Moon has high reliability.
- The Decision Definition is validated.
- Agreement is sufficient for production use.
- The study proves extraction accuracy.
- The study proves reviewer expertise.
- The study supports automatic extraction.
- The Decision Definition should be changed immediately.

## Reliability Posture

Evidence exists, but reliability is not validated.

The correct next step is to inspect disagreement and boundary cases before revising the definition or scaling extraction.
