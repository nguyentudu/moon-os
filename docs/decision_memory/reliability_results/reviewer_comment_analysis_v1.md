# Reviewer Comment Analysis v1

## Purpose

Cluster reviewer comments from the three Reliability Lab submissions.

## Comment Clusters

| Cluster | Example Comments | Evidence | Interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Agreement / acceptance comments | Ok; Đồng ý; Okie | Most reviewer comments use short acceptance language, especially on excerpts classified as decision by all reviewers. | Many excerpts felt straightforward, but short comments do not explain reasoning depth. |
| Comprehension uncertainty | Chưa hiểu; Chưa rõ; Chưa hiểu rõ; Chưa rõ nghĩa | Appears on EV-020, EV-021, EV-026, EV-028, EV-029, EV-031, EV-042, EV-043, EV-047. | Several excerpts lack enough local context for reviewers to classify confidently. |
| Rationale request | tại sao; Sao là con số đó; Nhưng mình muốn biết tại sao?; Tại sao | Appears on EV-027, EV-028, EV-033, EV-034. | Reviewers often treated missing rationale as ambiguity, especially around metrics, questions, and constraints. |
| Intent uncertainty | Không rõ ý đồ | Appears on EV-026. | Metric-like excerpts can be hard to classify when the intent of the statement is not visible. |
| Feasibility question | Nhưng có thể thực hiện được không | Appears on EV-033. | Some no-backend/no-submission boundaries may be read as implementation feasibility rather than decision evidence. |


## Evidence-Supported Observation

Reviewer comments suggest that ambiguity usually appears when the excerpt does not reveal rationale, intent, or consequence locally.

## Claim Boundary

Comment analysis is qualitative. It does not establish reviewer expertise, extraction accuracy, or production reliability.
