# Human Review Scorecard v1

## Purpose

Evaluate each proof artifact using human usefulness dimensions.

## Ratings

- Strong
- Acceptable
- Borderline
- Poor

## Scorecard

| Artifact | Clarity | Relevance | Trustworthiness | Explainability | Attention Worthiness | Emotional Safety | Agency Preservation | Overall Value |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Forgotten Commitment | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong | Acceptable | Strong | Strong |
| Goal Drift | Strong | Strong | Acceptable | Strong | Strong | Acceptable | Strong | Strong |
| Project Momentum Loss | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong | Strong |
| Relationship Decay | Acceptable | Borderline | Borderline | Acceptable | Borderline | Acceptable | Strong | Borderline |
| Learning Fragmentation | Strong | Acceptable | Strong | Strong | Borderline | Strong | Strong | Acceptable |

## Interpretation

Most artifacts are understandable and useful.

Relationship Decay is the most delicate and should be re-tested with extra trust review.

Learning Fragmentation is useful but should remain reflective rather than urgent.

## Non-Implementation Boundary

This scorecard is a human-review artifact only. It does not define product analytics, automated scoring, UI, integrations, notifications, reminders, or agents.
