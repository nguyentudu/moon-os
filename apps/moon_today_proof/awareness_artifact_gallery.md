# Awareness Artifact Gallery v1

## Purpose

Generate human-reviewable awareness artifacts for the five selected validated scenarios.

Only approved constitutional outcomes are used.

## Artifact 1: Forgotten Commitment

Title:

A commitment may need attention before its deadline

Summary:

An open commitment is still active, its deadline is near, and there has been no follow-up activity.

Why It Matters:

This may affect reliability, trust, and downstream responsibility.

Why Now:

The deadline is upcoming and no closure or follow-up evidence exists.

Evidence:

Commitment active; due date near; Person linked_to Commitment; no closure evidence.

Confidence:

High.

Uncertainty:

The commitment may have been handled outside the available evidence.

Trust Notes:

Low sensitivity. Present as awareness, not pressure.

Suggested Reflection:

Is this commitment still open, already handled, or ready to be renegotiated?

- docs/validation/commitment_continuity_cases.md

## Artifact 2: Goal Drift

Title:

An active goal may be drifting from recent choices

Summary:

The goal is still active, but calendar allocation is near zero and recent decisions appear misaligned.

Why It Matters:

This may indicate a gap between stated intention and current behavior.

Why Now:

Recent decisions affect the goal negatively while supporting projects are dormant.

Evidence:

Goal active; supporting projects dormant; decisions affect Goal negatively; Goal Drift high.

Confidence:

Moderate.

Uncertainty:

The drift may be intentional or reflect a changed priority not yet captured.

Trust Notes:

Frame without judgment. The user decides whether the goal still matters.

Suggested Reflection:

Does this goal still fit the current direction?

## Artifact 3: Project Momentum Loss

Title:

Project momentum may be fading

Summary:

An active project linked to an active goal has had no meaningful activity for 21 days.

Why It Matters:

The project may lose context or silently become abandoned while the goal remains active.

Why Now:

The inactivity period is long enough to create continuity risk.

Evidence:

Project active; Goal active; Project supports Goal; no meaningful activity for 21 days.

Confidence:

Moderate.

Uncertainty:

The pause may be intentional.

Trust Notes:

Low sensitivity. Avoid implying failure.

Suggested Reflection:

Is this project active, paused, or ready for closure?

## Artifact 4: Relationship Decay

Title:

A relationship thread may be quiet

Summary:

An important relationship has had no interaction for more than 90 days, but there is no direct evidence of decline.

Why It Matters:

Relationship continuity can fade without clear closure, but silence alone is not proof of a problem.

Why Now:

There is no strong today trigger, so this is better treated as a gentle watch item rather than an urgent item.

Evidence:

Relationship latent; prior active state; no explicit rupture evidence; Relationship Activity low.

Confidence:

Low to moderate.

Uncertainty:

Inactivity may be normal, intentional, or not meaningful.

Trust Notes:

Sensitive context. Do not surface details or create pressure.

Suggested Reflection:

Is this relationship something you want Moon to keep lightly in view?

Source References:

- docs/validation/relationship_continuity_cases.md
- docs/validation/execution/expected_vs_actual_matrix.md

## Artifact 5: Learning Fragmentation

Title:

Learning threads may be fragmenting

Summary:

Several learning topics have started but show no revisits, synthesis, or application.

Why It Matters:

Fragmented learning can make useful knowledge hard to recover later.

Why Now:

There is no urgent trigger, so this is more useful as reflection than immediate action.

Evidence:

Multiple LearningTopic nodes paused; Threads dormant; no Insight integration; Fragmentation Risk high.

Confidence:

Moderate.

Uncertainty:

Some learning may have been integrated outside the available evidence.

Trust Notes:

Low sensitivity. Avoid productivity pressure.

Suggested Reflection:
